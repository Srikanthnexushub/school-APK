ANSWER KEY

Q1: A — The block will start sliding when the component of gravity parallel to the incline equals the maximum static friction force. This gives mg sin θ = μ_s mg cos θ, which simplifies to tan θ = μ_s.
Q2: C — For a cyclic process, the change in internal energy is zero. By the first law of thermodynamics, ΔU = Q - W, so Q = W. Therefore, the net heat absorbed equals the net work done, which is 200 J.
Q3: C — Each +q charge exerts a force of magnitude kq²/a² on -2q. The angle between these two forces is 60°. Using vector addition, the net force is F_net = 2F cos 30° = 2(kq²/a²)(√3/2) = √3(kq²/a²). The magnitude is (4kq²)/a².
Q4: A — Using Ampere's law for an infinite straight wire, the magnetic field at distance r is given by B = μ₀I/(2πr), where μ₀ is the permeability of free space.
Q5: C — Using the mirror equation 1/f = 1/u + 1/v, where f = 20 cm and u = -30 cm, we get 1/20 = -1/30 + 1/v. Solving for v gives v = -60 cm. The negative sign indicates the image is formed 60 cm in front of the mirror.
Q6: A — The stopping potential is directly related to the maximum kinetic energy of photoelectrons by the equation K_max = eV_s, where e is the elementary charge and V_s is the stopping potential. Therefore, K_max = 1.6 × 10⁻¹⁹ C × 1.5 V = 2.4 × 10⁻¹⁹ J = 1.5 eV.
Q7: A — Centripetal acceleration is given by the formula a_c = v²/r, where v is the speed and r is the radius of the circular path.
Q8: D — For an adiabatic process, PV^γ = constant, where γ is the heat capacity ratio. If V₂ = V₁/2, then P₁V₁^γ = P₂V₂^γ = P₂(V₁/2)^γ. Solving for P₂ gives P₂ = 2^γ P₁.
Q9: C — The electric field due to each charge at point P has a component along the line joining the charges and a component perpendicular to it. The perpendicular components cancel out, while the parallel components add up. The magnitude of the net field is (2kqr/(r² + d²/4)^(3/2)), and it points along the line joining the charges (î direction).
Q10: A — The magnetic field inside a long solenoid is uniform and given by B = μ₀nI, where μ₀ is the permeability of free space, n is the number of turns per unit length, and I is the current flowing through the solenoid.
